Even in its purest form Tungsten is a very robust material that can maintain its integrity despite a variety of different forces and exertions. Young's modulus is a measurement of the stiffness of a material; measured in Pascals (Pa). The young's modulus of Tungsten is around 411,000,000 Pa or 411 GPa making it more than twice as stiff as steel. Compounding the stiffness of Tungsten is its hardness which is down to strong bonds between the atoms. To cut or indent Tungsten would require a very large amount of force. Tungsten's weakness culminate in its brittleness. Despite its hardness and stiffness there is a distinct lack of toughness in Tungsten. Crack propagation will occur if a large force is applied to a small area of the material.
